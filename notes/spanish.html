However, when we are talking about formal pronunciation, Spanish is not that similar to other languages. This note will focus on pronunciation tips, assuming that you already have a reasonable amount of knowledge about Spanish. To make this easier for non-native English speakers, sounds here have an IPA representation alongside its English-like sound.

Consonants

While I was diving into the /r/brasil subreddit, I found some interesting posts about language learning, so I will pretty much explain some things I already knew and some useful information I found in that post.

In contrast to English, Spanish's double-l (ll) is a digraph pronounced differently in many countries. Generally speaking about Latin American Spanish, it is usually pronounced just like English's y (/ʃ/). On the other hand, in Rioplatense-Spanish speaking countries (such as Uruguay and Argentina) it is pronounced as English's sh (/ʒ/), while in European Spanish it is pronounced as the English's j or ly (/ʎ/).

It is also worth noting that b and v consonant sounds in Spanish are identical in all dialects, and a similar situation happens with c, z, and s consonants, which are all pronounced just like the English's s (/s/) sound in Latin American Spanish, while in European Spanish, c and z are pronounced as English's non-voiced th (/θ/) sound, and s is pronounced as English's sh (/ʃ/).
